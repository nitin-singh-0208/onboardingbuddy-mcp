# RideFlex — Product Overview

## Vision (one paragraph)

RideFlex is a unified mobility platform that lets people move on demand through **cab-style rides** booked in minutes and **self-drive rentals** drawn from the same integrated fleet, with one account, one payment profile, and consistent safety and support standards. The product vision is to become the default way urban and suburban customers choose between “someone drives me” and “I drive myself” without juggling separate apps, contracts, or pickup logistics—while giving fleet operators and partner drivers a single operational surface for utilization, compliance, and earnings.

## Core problem

Consumers today typically use **different products** for point-to-point rides versus multi-hour or multi-day car access: separate sign-up, pricing models, insurance assumptions, and support channels. Fleet owners and aggregators lose **cross-sell opportunity** and suffer **fragmented dispatch**—vehicles may sit idle in one channel while demand spikes in another. RideFlex solves this by **pooling supply and demand** across ride-hailing and short-term rental, with shared vehicle records, driver/host roles, and a single booking lifecycle that branches based on product type (chauffeured trip vs. self-drive rental).

## Target users

| User type | Role | What they need from RideFlex |
|-----------|------|-------------------------------|
| **Riders** | End customers booking on-demand or scheduled chauffeured trips | Fast matching, transparent ETA and fare, in-trip safety tools, receipts, and support. |
| **Drivers** | Independent contractors or partners completing ride assignments | Steady trip offers, clear earnings, navigation and compliance prompts, and dispute resolution. |
| **Car-rental customers** | Users booking self-drive vehicles (hours to days) | Vehicle choice, pickup/return locations, add-ons (insurance, fuel), and clear handoff instructions. |
| **Fleet admins** | In-house or B2B operators managing vehicles, pricing zones, and utilization | Dashboards for fleet health, maintenance windows, geofencing rules, surge and rental yield, and regulatory reporting. |

## Product boundaries (fictional but realistic)

RideFlex **v1** in this documentation set focuses on **urban and suburban** trips under about two hours of chauffeured time, plus **self-drive rentals** from hours up to fourteen days. Long-distance intercity charters, corporate invoicing beyond a stub, and marketplace listing of third-party private cars are explicitly **out of scope** so onboarding exercises stay tractable. Internationalization exists as a concept—currency and unit formatting hooks—without pinning real countries or tax engines.

## Success themes (demo framing)

For onboarding purposes, treat RideFlex as **mid-scale**: multiple cities, a mix of owned and partner fleet, and regulatory posture that assumes standard background checks and insurance without naming real jurisdictions. All names, metrics, and integrations in companion documents are **illustrative only**.
